Use Case 1: AI-Accelerated R&D Formulation

In modern pharmaceuticals and beauty, time-to-market is dictated by wet-lab trial success rates. By utilizing Red Hat AI, enterprises can leverage decades of historical formulation data, microbiome interactions, and stability test results. InstructLab enables your formulation chemists—not just data scientists—to align open-source models using their domain knowledge, predicting compound viability in seconds before a physical trial even begins.

1. Domain Expertise

Chemists input historical stability data, pH balances, and target microbiome profiles into a secure repository.

2. InstructLab Alignment

The platform generates synthetic data to align an open-source model (like Granite) specifically to your biochemical taxonomy.

3. Predictive R&D

Lab technicians query the internal model to simulate compound stability, drastically reducing failed wet-lab trials.

Use Case 2: Precision Skincare Diagnostics

Consumer demand has shifted toward hyper-personalized health and beauty regimens. Capturing this market requires processing user PII (skin conditions, environmental data) against product catalogs in real-time. OpenShift AI, accelerated by the vLLM serving engine, allows you to integrate Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) directly into your consumer apps and edge devices. This delivers highly accurate product recommendations at scale, without the latency or exorbitant per-query costs of external APIs.

Consumer Edge App

User securely inputs skin type, age, and local environmental data.

vLLM Inference (Red Hat)

High-throughput, low-latency processing of the incoming diagnostic request.

Internal RAG Integration

Model strictly cross-references your validated proprietary product catalog.

Personalized Regimen

Consumer receives an accurate, safe, and brand-aligned product recommendation.

Objection Handling (Internal Use)

Objection: "We don't have a huge team of ML engineers."

Response: "That's exactly why Red Hat and IBM built InstructLab. It uses a taxonomy-driven approach where your domain experts (chemists, dermatologists) just input facts in plain text. The tool handles the synthetic data generation and fine-tuning automatically."

Objection: "Building our own infrastructure is too expensive vs API."

Response: "For initial experimentation, APIs are cheap. At production scale—serving consumer diagnostics globally—per-token costs skyrocket. Red Hat AI uses smaller, highly compressed models (Granite, Llama) running on optimized hardware, flattening your OpEx curve as you scale."

Objection: "Why not just use Databricks or Snowflake for this?"

Response: "Those are excellent data platforms, but they carry massive compute premiums for AI workloads and create vendor lock-in. OpenShift AI gives you an open-source standard. You can run it on AWS, Azure, or entirely bare-metal on-premises, keeping infrastructure control in your hands."
